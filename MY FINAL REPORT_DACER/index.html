On the Job Training

This document outlines my weekly progress and accomplishments during my On-the-Job Training (OJT) at Sydenham Laboratories Inc. (SLI). Throughout the training period, I was assigned to the MIS Department, where I contributed to the development of a company website. The following weekly summaries highlight the tasks, learning experiences, and technical skills I developed as part of this project.

WEEK 1

During my first week of On-the-Job Training (OJT) at SLI, I participated in an orientation program that included a tour of the company’s facilities. I was introduced to the various departments, company operations, and workflows, and was warmly welcomed by the employees, particularly the MIS team where I would be assigned. I observed and assisted in the repair and installation of essential hardware, including a thermal printer and scanner used in product processing. Although no formal tasks were assigned later in the week, I proactively used my time to begin learning Laravel, as advised by my supervisor, in preparation for a web development project I would be working on.

WEEK 2

I focused on creating the UI/UX design for the SLI (Sydenham Laboratories Inc.) project website using Figma. I began by researching design inspirations and developing initial wireframes, while consulting with my supervisor to align on functionality and content requirements. Then i refined the wireframes and designed the homepage layout. Midweek was dedicated to finalizing visual elements such as the color scheme, typography, and other design components. Overall, this week enabled me to establish the website’s visual structure while ensuring consistency with the company’s branding and objectives.

WEEK 3

During Week 3, I focused on refining the website’s design to achieve a more structured and visually cohesive layout. Collaborating with my co-intern, Christian, we reviewed and enhanced the UI/UX design while coordinating with our supervisor to discuss additional content requirements. We worked on improving navigation and overall user experience, and finalized key pages such as “Contact Us,” “Products,” and others. By the end of the week, we conducted a comprehensive review of all UI/UX elements and presented the updated design for feedback on Friday.

WEEK 4

Together with my fellow intern, I continued working on the UI/UX design of the project website using Figma. We refined the layout and interface to ensure consistency and usability. On Tuesday and Wednesday, while my co-intern is working on designing, I began developing the website structure through coding, making sure it aligned with the required specifications. On Thursday, we were assigned to assist Sir Edwin and Sir Kyle with setting up LAN cables and computer units in the Warehouse Office. On Valentine’s Day, we continued cataloging all the company's products to be showcased on the website.

WEEK 5

During Week 5, I focused on the coding phase of the website from Monday to Wednesday, translating the approved UI/UX design into functional web development. On Thursday, we held a brief meeting and presented the Figma web design to Sir Tony, the CSG General Manager, who provided valuable insights and feedback regarding the website's structure and functionality. On Friday, I worked on implementing the necessary adjustments and improvements based on the feedback received.

WEEK 6

During Week 6, I continued developing the Laravel project, focusing on key structural components. On Monday, I worked on building the foundational elements of the website, including the top bar, navigation bar, footer, and setting up essential controllers. On Tuesday, I refined these components and ensured proper linkage between controllers and their respective views. On Wednesday, I focused on updating visual content by reviewing and replacing outdated images to improve the overall appearance and relevance of the website.

WEEK 7

From Monday to Wednesday, we focused on coding the website, ensuring that its functionality aligned with the intended design and requirements. On Friday, we conducted a meeting with department heads and associates to present the current website layout. The presentation was well-received, and the feedback provided was positive, reflecting satisfaction with the progress and design direction.

WEEK 8

We focused on refining and enhancing the website project based on the feedback received from the previous meeting. From Monday to Tuesday, we implemented the necessary improvements discussed, ensuring better functionality and user experience. On Wednesday, we redesigned several website pages to enhance their layout and overall UX. By Thursday, I completed the CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) functionalities for key components such as products, news, categories, hiring sections and etc.

WEEK 9

This week i accomplished several key tasks related to the website project. From Monday to Tuesday, I focused on backend development, specifically completing functionalities that required database integration. By Friday, I successfully finalized the setup, enabling users to post and manage job listings within the system.

WEEK 10

From Monday to Wednesday, we finalized adjustments to the design of various web pages to improve user-friendliness. This involved refining layouts, improving navigation, and optimizing the interface for a more intuitive and accessible user experience changes that were successfully applied to the Laravel-based website. On Thursday, we began developing the “Contact Us” form using Laravel’s email functionality. This included setting up the form structure, integrating it with the backend, and configuring validations to ensure proper data handling. By Friday, the Contact Us form was functional and successfully sending emails through Laravel. However, additional improvements are planned to enhance its efficiency, security, and overall user experience.

WEEK 11

During Week 11, I focused on enhancing the News section of the website. From Monday to Wednesday, I worked on modifying its structure to support multiple category assignments for each news item, replacing the previous single-category limitation. On Friday, I completed the UI/UX design for the News section. The layout was finalized and improved to provide better user interaction and readability, ensuring a more engaging and accessible experience for users.

WEEK 12

From Monday to Tuesday, I resolved technical issues in the Contact Form, focusing on SSL encryption and TLS certificate integration to enhance data security. On Thursday, I studied how to implement web analytics to track user activity through the admin panel. On Friday, I worked on integrating and refining the tracking features to ensure proper functionality.

WEEK 13

On Monday, I completed the implementation of analytics for the admin dashboard, enabling tracking of website viewing activity. On Tuesday, we conducted a pre-testing session using the domain name to ensure proper functionality, accessibility, and responsiveness across various screen sizes. By Wednesday, I finalized mobile integration to enhance responsiveness. Despite the Holy Week, I continued improving these features on Thursday and Friday.

WEEK 14

From Tuesday to Wednesday, I focused on finalizing all website functionalities and requirements, ensuring every feature was completed and thoroughly tested. On Thursday and Friday, I proceeded with live hosting the website using an IP address. The deployment process was completed smoothly, and the website is now fully accessible online through the assigned IP.

WEEK 15

This week, I had no assigned tasks as I had already completed all project deliverables for the website. I used this time to review the finished product, ensuring everything was properly organized and ready for final submission. As I officially wrap up my OJT journey, I would like to express my sincere gratitude to my supervisor and co-intern for their guidance, support, and encouragement throughout this valuable learning experience.
